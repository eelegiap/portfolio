About me:

I'm a May 2023 graduate from Harvard who studied Computer Science and Slavic Languages and Literatures. When I'm not agonizing over Jupyter Notebooks or parsing large textual datasets, you can find me reading Dostoevsky or trying to pet the neighborhood cats. I pursue research at the intersection of language, computation, and media studies, by using tools from natural language processing, data analytics, and media & literary theory.


Positions:

  • Former researcher on online Russian disinformation with the Social Media Lab at Stanford's Center for the Study of Language and Information.
  • Former researcher with Prof. Amelia Glaser at the Harvard Radcliffe Institute for Advanced Study on a project creating an archive of Ukrainian poetry posted to Facebook after 2014.
  • Former researcher with Prof. Steven Clancy on his Visualizing Russian project. Feel free to check it out!
  • Former president of the Harvard Technology Review


Publications:

  • Visualizing Russian: Illuminating corpora, conjugations, and classrooms, Russian Language Journal, Dec. 2022: Article on digital resource developed for Russian language learners, language teachers, and linguistic scholars.
